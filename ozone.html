The Ozone Layer

Protective Layer

Absorbs 97-99% of the Sun's harmful UV radiation

Chemical Composition

O₃ molecules in stratosphere (15-35km altitude)

Antarctic Vulnerability

Unique conditions accelerate ozone destruction

The Ozone Hole

Discovery

First detected in 1985 by British scientists at Halley Station

Mechanism

Polar stratospheric clouds enable chlorine destruction of ozone

Seasonal Pattern

Forms each Antarctic spring (Sept-Nov) and recovers by summer

Ozone Hole Size Over Time

Montreal Protocol

The 1987 Montreal Protocol on Substances that Deplete the Ozone Layer:

  • First UN treaty to achieve universal ratification
  • Phased out 99% of ozone-depleting chemicals
  • Prevented an estimated 2 million annual skin cancer cases
  • Ozone layer expected to recover by mid-21st century

CFC Reduction

98% decrease in ozone-depleting substances since 1980s

Health Benefits

Prevented 280 million cases of skin cancer by 2100

Environmental Impact

Ozone layer recovering at 1-3% per decade
