Scientific Research

Antarctica has no indigenous economy - all activity is supported by national governments and scientific organizations:

Annual Research Expenditures

United States

$350-400 million

European Nations

$250-300 million

Other Countries

$150-200 million

Key Research Areas

  • Climate change and ice sheet dynamics
  • Astronomy and astrophysics (clean air and darkness)
  • Marine biology and ecosystems
  • Geology and paleontology
  • Space analog research

Regulation and Sustainability

IAATO

International Association of Antarctica Tour Operators sets strict guidelines

Antarctic Treaty

Requires environmental impact assessments for all activities

CCAMLR

Manages marine resources in Southern Ocean

The Madrid Protocol designates Antarctica as a "natural reserve devoted to peace and science" with strict environmental protection measures.
